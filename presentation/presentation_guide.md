# Apex Sales Performance Presentation Guide
**Internal Sales Organization (ISO) Business Intelligence Analyst Candidate Presentation**

This document serves as your slide-by-slide strategy and transcript notes for the 20-minute presentation to Sales and Leadership stakeholders. It addresses all requirements outlined in the case study.

*   **Slide 1: Title & Executive Summary (2 Mins)** — The growth problem and the 7% growth target.

*   **Slide 3: Sales Performance Analytics (5 Mins)** — Funnel leakage and productivity breakdowns.

## 🖼️ Slide-by-Slide Presentation Outline & Speaker Notes

### Slide 1: Title & Executive Summary
*   **Slide Header:** *Driving Growth Through Insight: Turning Sales Data into Revenue Levers*
*   **Visuals:** Current Gap Gauge Chart showing current growth (1.5% YoY) vs. the 2026 Target (7.0%).
*   **Key Points:**
    *   **The Diagnosis:** Apex's growth is stagnant due to a heavy reliance on existing account expansion (Farmers) and low visibility into net-new business acquisition (Hunters).
    *   **The Mandate:** Shift from reactive, inconsistent reporting to a proactive, data-driven sales organization.
*   **Speaker Notes:** 
    > *"Good morning everyone. Today, we aren't just looking at charts—we are establishing a roadmap to drive 7% revenue growth. Currently, Apex is experiencing stagnant 1.5% growth. Our biggest vulnerability is that we are coasting on existing customer accounts (Farmers), while our pipeline for net-new business (Hunters) is leaking. Today I will show you how we will use Power BI and Salesforce data to find where we are dropping the ball and how we can close the gap."*

---

### Slide 2: Data Strategy & Standardized KPIs
*   **Slide Header:** *Standardizing the Metrics: One Source of Truth*
*   **Visuals:** A table showing KPI definitions across the sales lifecycle.
*   **Standardized Metric Framework:**
    1.  **Pipeline Health:**
        *   *Weighted Pipeline Value:* Expected revenue based on win probabilities of active deals.
        *   *Pipeline Coverage Ratio:* Active pipeline value divided by the sales target (recommended target: 3x).
    2.  **Conversion Efficiency:**
        *   *Stage-to-Stage Transition Rate:* Percentage of opportunities moving from Prospecting -> Qualification -> Proposal -> Negotiation -> Closed Won.
        *   *Win Rate:* Closed Won Deals / Total Closed Deals.
    3.  **Sales Activity:**
        *   *Activity Velocity:* Sum of Calls, Emails, and Meetings logged per opportunity per week.
    4.  **Revenue Quality:**
        *   *New Logo (Hunter) vs. Expansion (Farmer) Ratio:* Total USD won from net-new accounts vs. upsell/cross-sell.
*   **Speaker Notes:**
    > *"To solve stagnant growth, we must speak the same language. We are standardizing four KPI categories: Pipeline, Conversion, Activity, and Revenue. By linking Salesforce Opportunities directly to Power BI, we will track Pipeline Coverage to ensure our reps have at least 3x their target in their pipeline. More importantly, we will separate Hunter and Farmer revenue so leadership can see exactly how much of our growth is net-new vs. expansion."*

---

### Slide 3: Sales Performance Analytics: Differentiating Roles
*   **Slide Header:** *Hunters vs. Farmers: Right Metrics for the Right Roles*
*   **Visuals:** Role-based dashboard wireframe.
*   **Reporting Breakdown:**
    *   **Inside Sales (Hunters):** Focus on **top-of-funnel activity and velocity**.
        *   *Key KPIs:* Outbound calls logged, first-meetings booked, Prospecting-to-Qualification conversion, Average days in pipeline.
    *   **Account Management (Farmers):** Focus on **retention, account health, and expansion size**.
        *   *Key KPIs:* Renewal rate, Net Revenue Retention (NRR), Cross-sell pipeline, average contract value growth.
    *   **Sales Support:** Focus on **enablement and turnaround time**.
        *   *Key KPIs:* Proposal generation speed, contract turnaround time, win rate support index.
*   **Speaker Notes:**
    > *"A common mistake is measuring all sales reps with the same yardstick. Hunters (Inside Sales) are focused on pipeline creation—so we measure outbound activity and early-stage conversions. Farmers (Account Managers) are measured on renewal and expansion rates. If a Hunter's deal is stalled in 'Proposal' stage, we look at Sales Support turnaround times. This role-based reporting helps pinpoint exactly where the bottlenecks are."*

---

### Slide 4: Strategic Levers: Hitting the 7% Growth Target
*   **Slide Header:** *The Math Behind the 7%: What Levers Do We Pull?*
*   **Visuals:** Funnel chart showing the current leakage points vs. optimized funnel.
*   **Actionable Growth Levers (How to achieve the target):**
    1.  **The New Logo Opportunity:** Currently, Hunter win rates are low (~10-15%) and activity volume is low. If we increase Hunter outbound call activity by **15%**, it generates more top-of-funnel opportunities.
    2.  **Plugging the Proposal Leak:** The data shows deals are stalling at the *Proposal* stage. By improving the proposal-to-negotiation transition rate by just **5%** (via automated Sales Support templates and faster RFPs), we increase overall win rates.
    3.  **Expanding Account Size:** A **2% increase** in average Farmer deal size through structured cross-selling closes the remaining deficit.
*   **Speaker Notes:**
    > *"How do we get to 7%? It's a combination of volume and efficiency. By analyzing our funnel, we found that our biggest leakage happens at the Proposal stage. Rushing proposals or delayed support causes deals to freeze. If we improve our Proposal conversion rate by just 5% and increase Hunter outbound outreach by 15%, the compounding math hits our 7% growth goal. The Growth Simulator in our Power BI prototype demonstrates this dynamically."*

---

### Slide 5: Risks, Mitigations & Success Criteria
*   **Slide Header:** *Ensuring Adoption: Technology is Only Half the Battle*
*   **Visuals:** Risk matrix diagram.
*   **Implementation Strategy:**
    *   **Risk 1: Bad Salesforce Data (Garbage In, Garbage Out):** Reps don't log calls or update opportunity stages.
        *   *Mitigation:* Automate activity logging via Outlook/Gmail integrations. Introduce a Power BI "Hygiene Scorecard" showing which managers have the most outdated opportunities.
    *   **Risk 2: Low Adoption:** Sales leadership continues using static spreadsheets.
        *   *Mitigation:* Conduct role-based training and deprecate manual reports. Incorporate dashboard metrics directly into weekly forecast meetings.
    *   **Success Metrics for BI Analyst:**
        *   Adoption rate of the Power BI report (Weekly active users).
        *   Reduction in average days an opportunity remains in a stale stage.
        *   Increase in data accuracy score.
*   **Speaker Notes:**
    > *"A dashboard is useless if it's not adopted. The two biggest risks are poor Salesforce hygiene and low report adoption. To mitigate bad data, we will implement automatic activity tracking and launch a 'Pipeline Hygiene Scorecard'. Success for me as your BI Analyst means that 90% of our sales managers use this report weekly to run their forecasting meetings, moving us from gut-feeling sales to data-backed revenue generation. Thank you, and I'd love to take your questions."*
